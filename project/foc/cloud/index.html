Introduction to Cloud

Ever heard of Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube or Netflix.

If yes, then congratulations you are already familiar with cloud. These tech-giants use cloud service providers to provide a better service to their users. Some examples of cloud Storage are DropBox, Google Drive, One Drive

Let's define Cloud

Cloud computing refers to the delivery of on-demand computing services over the internet as a pay-as-you-go basis. OR,

Rather than managing files and services on a local storage device, you'll be doing the same over the internet.

Giving Example

Let's say you are an entrepreneur with millions of customers and thousands of workers under you. How are you going to manage them? Of course, you have to maintain daily records of your workers as well as customers. Like daily attendance of workers, their weekly progress and many more. In the case of your customers, you need to make a record of their interest, what is their area of interest, which part of the group of consumers like what kind of product. You need a database to maintain this large amount of data. But creating a database at your own place may be expensive and unsecure. I mean you create your own database then you have to maintain it throughout the year even when you don't need it like during holidays, and there is a chance of being hacked by someone. Ohh! it's kinda messy don't you think? Here comes the Cloud into picture.

Everything at once

So, rather than managing files and data on our own, we can use cloud services provided by cloud service providers.
Now the question is why should we provide our valuable data to other companies and pay them for keeping our data.

    Advantages of Cloud:

  • Large data can be managed and stored easily.
  • You have full control over your data even with no code.
  • Your data is private, can be accessed only to those whom you prefer.
  • The main advantage of using cloud over an offline database is that it can be used from anywhere around the world.
  • Cloud provides hyper-secure file share.
  • When using cloud services you only need to pay as you use the service.

ML in Cloud

Cloud plays a crucial role in Machine Learning.
SGE, Bard, GPT-4 provide response through the cloud.

Machine learning typically involves 3 stages:

  • Build
  • Train
  • Predict

Cloud handles the training aspect for you. Major cloud providers like AWS, GCP and Azure have various tools for Machine learning like vertex AI, Sage maker.

There are various cloud based jupyter notebooks for Machine learning like Colab and Kaggle by Google where you can simply upload your data and train a custom model for prediction
