Introduction to Cloud

Ever heard of Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube or Netflix.

If yes, then congratulations you are already familiar with cloud. These tech-gaints uses cloud service providers in order to provide a better service to their users.\nSome of Examples of cloud Storage are DropBox, Google Drive, One Drive

Let's define Cloud

Cloud computing refers to the delevery of on-demand computing services over the internet as a pay-as-you-go basis. OR,

Rather than managing files and services on a local storage device you'll be doing the same over the internet.

Giving Example

Let's say you are an enterprenuar with a millions of customers and a thousands of workers under you. How are you going to manage them? Ofcourse you have to maintain a daily records of your workers as well as customers. Like daily attendance of workers, their weekly progress and many more. in case of your customers you need to make a record of their interest, what is their area of interest which part of group of consumers like what kind of product.You need a database to maintain these large amount of data. but creating a database at your ownplace may be expensive and unsecure. I mean you create your own database then you have to maintain it throughout the year even when you dont need it like during holidays, and there is a chance of being hacked by someone. ohh! its kinda messy dont you think? Here comes the Cloud into picture.

Everything at once

So, rather than managing files and datas on our own we can use cloud services provided by cloud service providers.
Now the questions is why should we provide our valuable data to other companies and pay them for keeping our data.

    Advantages of Cloud:

  • Large data can be managed and stored easily.
  • You have full control over your data even with no code.
  • Your data is private, can be accessed only to those whom you prefer.
  • The main advantage of using cloud over offline database is that it can be used from anywhere around the world.
  • Cloud provide a hyper security file share.
  • When using cloud services you only need to pay as you use the service.
